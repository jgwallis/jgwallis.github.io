The way I work

Buying equipment and parts

In the past I’ve bought hardware on behalf of customers and then been stuck with it when they changed their minds about having work done. Therefore I no longer purchase items for customers, but instead just advise them what they need and let them obtain it.

This policy has several practical advantages for customers:

  • Because I’m not spending time ordering items and waiting for them to be delivered, travelling to a shop and buying them in person, or taking the risk of being lumbered with things that I don’t need, I don’t have to pass on any additional costs, and customers end up paying less
  • Customers can be absolutely sure that I’m not making any money out of selling them items at grossly inflated prices (I wouldn’t do that anyway, but there are certainly people in the computer industry who would)
  • If an item fails during its warranty period, it is easier for customers to return it if the paperwork has their details on it rather than mine (many warranties only last as long as the original purchaser owns the item, and if I were to buy the item and sell it on, straight away such a warranty would become useless)

So don’t pay mark-up on parts, just buy what I recommend then only pay for fitting.

Real on-site support

If you ask me a simple question on the telephone then I’ll probably be able to answer it without arranging a visit, but for anything else I’ll usually come to see you in person. I don’t do ‘remote support’ over the Internet for several reasons, not the least of which is the potential security risk of running the necessary software. However, this is not an excuse to charge a call-out fee (see my policy on callout fees).

Free collection and delivery within five miles

If I can’t reasonably fix a problem on-site, I’ll gladly take equipment away to be fixed, and bring it back again, without making a call-out charge for nearby customers. There aren’t many computer support businesses who’ll do that, so this is a good reason to get in touch with me for jobs such as computer repairs and laptop repairs.

Sensitive data

If I need to take away a computer or laptop for repair, I can always remove the hard disk drive first and leave it with you, if it contains sensitive data that you don’t want taken off your premises.

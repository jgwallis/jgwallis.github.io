Prices

For the year from 6th April 2016 to 5th April 2017, my basic rate for computer troubleshooting is £28 per hour with a minimum chargeable time of one hour. After the first hour, time is charged in quarter-hour chunks, with any part of a quarter-hour counting as a whole quarter-hour. So one-and-a-quarter hours of work is £35, one hour and twenty-five minutes is £42, and so on.

There is no call-out fee for local work, but there is a half-hour charge (£14) for any job that is more than five miles from my home, as the crow flies. I have to charge this to account for the time it takes to drive both ways, fuel, business insurance, and sometimes parking.

Charges for tuition are considerably lower. Please get in touch for further details.

No fix, no fee

If I am called out to solve a specific problem and find that I cannot do so, I do not charge anything whatsoever. If, however, I am called out to solve a problem and are successful, but am then asked to look at another problem which I cannot solve, I still charge for fixing the initial problem.

‘No fix, no fee’ means that if I cannot solve a problem then I will not charge. However, if a potential customer does not give me a chance to fix whatever problem I have found then there will be a small diagnosis fee (equivalent to half-an-hour’s time). I had to introduce this policy shortly after starting my business because sometimes a potential customer would call me out to find out what was wrong with their computer, then decide to not bother fixing it, or to fix it themselves, or to get somebody else to fix it.

Being reasonable

If you call me out to do a job and it turns out to be very quick and simple, I will only ask for a small, reasonable payment. In other words, I will not rigidly stick to my normal charges if doing so would be ridiculously unfair. A lot of my work is repeat business for customers who appreciate my reasonable approach to charging. So you can call me out without worrying about how much it is going to cost.

Payment

I accept payment in cash, or by cheque. Unfortunately I do not have the facility to process credit or debit card payments.
